# Target Audience & User Personas

WanderNest serves two distinct user groups: **property operators** (buyers) and **guests** (end users). The product must resonate with guests first, because guest delight is what sells it to properties.

---

## Primary Market: Property Types

### 1. Wellness Retreats & Spas
**Why they need WanderNest:**
- Guests want to slow down and explore mindfully
- Properties have meditation spots, nature trails, gardens that tell a story
- Audio guides complement the wellness experience
- Non-intrusive way to guide guests without staff intervention

**Example hotspots:** Meditation garden, forest bathing trail, sunrise viewpoint, herb garden with wellness properties

### 2. Safari Lodges & Wildlife Reserves
**Why they need WanderNest:**
- Vast grounds with scattered points of interest
- Safety zones and restricted areas need clear marking
- Educational content about wildlife, ecology, conservation
- Guests explore between guided activities

**Example hotspots:** Watering hole viewing deck, bird hide, historical site, conservation project info point

### 3. Luxury Resorts & Hotels
**Why they need WanderNest:**
- Large properties with hidden amenities
- Guests often underutilize resort features
- Storytelling opportunity for heritage properties
- Differentiator for upscale positioning

**Example hotspots:** Secret beach access, historical chapel, chef's garden, art installation, ocean-view terrace

### 4. Glamping & Eco-Resorts
**Why they need WanderNest:**
- Nature-focused guests who want to explore
- Properties built around environmental features
- Off-grid areas where offline support matters
- Educational mission around sustainability

**Example hotspots:** Solar field explainer, local flora walk, stargazing spot, wildlife corridor

### 5. Vineyards & Estate Properties
**Why they need WanderNest:**
- Walking tours are core to the experience
- Educational content about production process
- Tasting spots, scenic overlooks, historic buildings
- Self-guided as an alternative to staff-led tours

**Example hotspots:** Fermentation cellar, original farmhouse, oak barrel aging room, hilltop tasting deck

## Buyer Personas

### Persona A: "Experience Director Emma"
**Role:** Guest Experience Manager at a 50-room wellness resort

**Goals:**
- Reduce repetitive questions from guests ("Where is the spa?")
- Create memorable moments without adding staff
- Differentiate from competitors
- Extend engagement beyond the main activities

**Pain points:**
- Limited budget for technology
- Can't require guests to download an app
- Needs something easy to set up and maintain
- Worried about technical complexity

**What wins her over:**
- No app download for guests
- Can set up in an afternoon
- Demo mode lets her try before committing
- Mobile QR flow for adding hotspots on-site

---

### Persona B: "Operations-Focused Owen"
**Role:** General Manager at a safari lodge

**Goals:**
- Keep guests entertained between game drives
- Communicate safety information passively
- Showcase conservation efforts
- Reduce reliance on printed materials

**Pain points:**
- Remote location with inconsistent connectivity
- Staff spread thin across a large property
- Guests speak multiple languages
- Budget pressures from ownership

**What wins him over:**
- Offline capability
- One-time setup, low maintenance
- Works on any smartphone
- QR code distribution is simple

---

### Persona C: "Brand-Builder Bella"
**Role:** Marketing Director at a luxury resort group

**Goals:**
- Elevate the brand experience
- Create shareable moments for social media
- Gather data on guest behavior
- Roll out across multiple properties

**Pain points:**
- Needs to justify ROI
- Wants custom branding, not generic tech
- Concerns about data privacy
- Requires scalability

**What wins her over:**
- Premium aesthetic matches brand
- Privacy-first approach (no tracking)
- Analytics on engagement (future feature)
- Embed option for existing app integration

## End User Personas (Guests)

### Persona D: "Curious Traveler Clara"
**Profile:** Solo traveler, 30s, values authentic experiences

**Behavior:**
- Prefers self-guided exploration over tours
- Uses phone for travel research but hates app bloat
- Seeks hidden gems and local stories
- Enjoys walking and photography

**What delights her:**
- Discovering a spot not in the brochure
- Audio content while walking (no screen staring)
- Privacy (not being tracked)
- Beautiful, uncluttered interface

---

### Persona E: "Family Organizer Felix"
**Profile:** Traveling with kids, wants to keep everyone engaged

**Behavior:**
- Needs activities that work for all ages
- Values anything that gets kids off screens outdoors
- Time-constrained; wants efficient discovery
- Trusts property recommendations

**What delights him:**
- Gamified feel ("let's find the next spot")
- Audio content kids can listen to
- Map shows what's nearby
- Works without cell service at remote properties

---

### Persona F: "Relaxation-Seeker Rosa"
**Profile:** Wellness retreat guest, wants calm and minimal friction

**Behavior:**
- Avoiding screens but uses phone when necessary
- Looking for peaceful spots to be alone
- Appreciates guidance without intrusion
- Values simplicity above all

**What delights her:**
- Not having to ask staff for directions
- Serene, minimal interface
- Audio guides she can close her eyes and listen to
- Auto-discovery (content just appears)

---

## Market Positioning

**We are:** A GPS-powered exploration tool for hospitality properties that transforms guest experience through discovery-based content delivery.

**We are not:**
- A generic tour guide app
- A wayfinding or navigation system
- A guest services app (ordering, booking, etc.)
- A gamified scavenger hunt (though that's possible)

**Key differentiators:**
1. No app download required
2. Works offline
3. Privacy-first (location stays on device)
4. Property-specific, not a marketplace
5. Premium aesthetic that matches hospitality brands

## Geographic Focus

### Phase 1: Local Testing (Current)

**UK — Somerset & Southwest England**

Primary focus for initial testing, demos, and early client acquisition. Benefits:
- Proximity for in-person demos and onboarding
- Dense concentration of target property types
- Manageable travel for site visits

**Somerset property types:**
- Boutique hotels and country house hotels
- Glamping sites and eco-lodges
- Wellness retreats and spas
- Farm stays and rural estates
- Vineyards and cider farms
- Wedding venues with grounds

**Nearby expansion:**
- Devon, Dorset, Wiltshire (similar property density)
- Cotswolds (luxury country hotels, estates)
- Welsh borders (outdoor adventure properties)

### Phase 2: UK National

Once product-market fit is validated locally:
- Lake District, Peak District (outdoor/adventure)
- Scottish Highlands (lodges, estates)
- Cornwall (coastal resorts, wellness)

### Phase 3: International (Future)

- Western Europe — Vineyards, estate hotels
- Southern Africa — Safari lodges, eco-resorts
- Southeast Asia — Wellness retreats, luxury resorts

English-first interface throughout all phases.
